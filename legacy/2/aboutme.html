I grew up in Beachwood, Ohio (a suburb of Cleveland). Currently, I am a second year Computer Science and Engineering student at The Ohio State University. My program includes a specialization in software engineering. I am eager to continue learning and creating projects. I am looking to gain valuable experience through a summer internship program. I am passionate about software development and technology as a whole.

I have been interested in technological advancements for as long as I can remember, but my interests really expanded when I took AP Computer Science in my sophomore year of high school. This class very quickly became my favorite and it was my first experience in programming. After such a positive experience, I went on to take a couple more programming classes: Honors Software Engineering and Honors Robotics. I also became a member of the Robotics team with a role in programming our robot. I joined our Science Olympiad team with an event leader role in my favorite event: Game On, in which a pair had to create a game using Scratch (a new type of programming to me). I also joined our Science and Engineering Fair Club. Through this club, I created an iPhone App and a Java program on my own and won awards. More recently, I have completed coursework and projects at The Ohio State University. I have also created another iPhone App in my own personal time. I also created an iPhone App at the 2018 Hack Ohi/o hackathon in a team of three. All of these projects have impacted my knowledge, interests, and experience. My passion for software development has grown constantly since taking AP Computer Science and it is still continuing to grow with every project I complete.

I am extremely excited to continue learning at The Ohio State University, complete personal projects, and gain experience through an internship. I am motivated to earn one of these available opportunities. After graduating with my degree, I hope to work as a software developer. Eventually, I would like to create my own technology/software company.
